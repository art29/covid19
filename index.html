OVERVIEW

In the past few weeks we've been working with different medical professionals to create a Face Shield that would help them in their different tasks. With 3D Printers, CNC Machines and other equipment we can make hundreds of Face Shields a day with the help of our dedicated community of volunteers !

ORDER FACE SHIELDS

If you need PPE face shields for your hospital or for other medical institutions, tell us using the form below !

Our team works with medical professionals to improve the face shields every day ! We now have a final design that is Health Canada approved, which works really well for medical personnel using it ! We will try to do our best to get them to you as fast as possible, but please note that it might take a few days to process and print the face shields.

HOW CAN YOU HELP ?

First, the easiest thing that you can do to help with the COVID-19 pandemic is to stay home unless you absolutely need to. If you need to please follow the social distancing policies (2 meters or 6 feet from everyone).

Another way you can help out if you have a 3D Printer is to print some parts for the Face Shields. The steps are available below.

Steps for 3D Printing

  1. Download the STL File (Please print the Velcro Version right now) and make sure you can print it (Dimension, Quality ...).
  2. Print them while making sure you use gloves when touching the parts so they are NOT contaminated. Please also check that the print is free of cracks and that quality is acceptable !
  3. Use this form to let us know that you printed some and how we should get them (Pickup or Drop Off)
  4. You can now give them to us by one of these two options :
    • We can pick them up : We will coordinate with volunteers to come, we will send you a pickup time, you will need to put the box outside to reduce human interactions.
    • You can drop them off between 11am and 8pm daily, We will have a box at the main entrance of STEM Complex (160 Louis-Pasteur Private). If you want to drop off another time please send an email to Midia (mshikhha@uottawa.ca).
  5. We will then assemble them and send them to different medical facilities in the region !
  6. Thanks for your help ! If you want to do more, go ahead, we will be more than happy to get some help !
